Brief Bio

I study Computer Science at Stony Brook University, and after my M.S, I plan on pursuing a PhD. My research interests include computer architecture, operating systems, virtualization, compilers, security, and programming languages. I am a graduate of the class of 2020, with a B.S. in Computer Science.

I am a research assistant in the Exascallab, headed by Dr. Barbara Chapman, where I will be conducting research in High performance Computing and the behavior of different processors and toolkits in the context of multi-node clusters.

I am also an active member of the Spirit of Stony Brook Marching Band, where I was one of the drum line section leaders, focusing on marching snare (2017-2020). As an incoming graduate student, I no longer hold that title, though I still play as part of the snare line.


I was a member of Stony Brook's COMPAS Lab, under the direction of Dr. Mike Ferdman intermittently between 2018 and the start of 2020, where my latest project focused on cloud computing benchmarks and measurements of performance metrics to improve quality of service (QoS). My past CS-related positions include a summer BAE Systems as a software intern and the Stony Brook CS Department as a Teaching Assistant for the 2019 calendar year.


In summer 2019, I had the honor of receiving funding from the International Symposium on Computer Architecture (ISCA) to attend the first of hopefully many undergraduate Architecture (uArch) workshops. It was an extremely humbling experience, and has broadened my view of computer architecture and research, as well as meeting some very interesting people in the CS community.


In my spare time, I practice Tae-Kwon-Do, a Korean martial art in which I've received a 4th degree black belt, achieving the title of "Master." I can also be found practicing drums, learning new things in computer science and/or mathematics, and practicing more drums.
