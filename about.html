About Me: An Auto-Hagiography

I hail from Park Ridge, New Jersey, a small town in Bergen County. The transition from such a small school district to something as large as Stony Brook University was something very eye-opening for me, and very intimidating as well. I was very lucky to start my college experience off with the Spirit of Stony Brook Marching Band. For example, I started my Fall 2016 semester knowing only that one other person from Park Ridge attended this university, and since then, I've developed a phenomenal network of friends (mainly members in the drum-line and computer science communities). They've seen me at my best and my worst, and I'm happy to have them at my back.



Before becoming heavily interested and invested in Computer Science and Mathematics, I initially had the intention to attend college for music, as I've been playing percussion since 2007. That endeavor ended for two reasons: One of them was because I stumbled upon an introductory programming class and instantly fell in love with it; the other was because, at the time, I thought I'd be going to school for something that I already know how to do. In addition. Luckily at Stony Brook University, I can keep both my love of music and my love of programming and Computer Science.



Outside of academia and drumming, I practice Tae-Kwon-Do, a Korean martial art, and have been since 2004. I have kept a steady pace towards bettering myself in the art. It's changed for me over the years, starting out as something that looked cool while watching television to a way to better myself as a person, teach me discipline, etc. Through this way of thinking and acting, I have progressed to a 4th degree black belt (the rank of "Master"), and at this rank, I have gained enough knowledge to be able to teach other students the theory and concepts of Tae-Kwon-Do, which I do when I'm at home for a break or for the summer. It's teachings are really applicable to many other aspects of life, which is pretty cool as well.
